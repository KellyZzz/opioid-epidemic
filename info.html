How Information is Calculated

To calculate the MyRisk, an individual must input 3 variables: gender, age-range, and ethnicity. In order to calculate the total probability of one of the variables, we must first calculate the total amount of deaths for a variable range divided by the total deaths for the state of California. After calculating the probability for each of the 3 variables, the variables must be multiplied together; this calculates the total probability. The total population for individuals who fulfill the 3 variables, is then determined. Using this 3-variable specific population, the total deaths are then divided by that specific population and the overall risk for the individual is calculated.

Where the Data is Collected From

Data for all the information visualizations used for the Analytics page is collected from the California Department of Public Health Database. The analytics data has been collected and stored into CSV files, which can be downloaded below.

Data for all of the MyRisk Section, such as populations for gender, ethnicity, and age groups, was collected from the US Census Database
